Here's the honest, encouraging truth: the theory half of this exam is a memory-and-vocabulary game, not a math game. The professor reuses the same ~40 ideas over and over across every past exam. Learn those ideas once — the way you'd learn the offside rule — and the multiple choice and definition questions become nearly automatic.

This guide uses the World Cup as one big analogy machine. A variable is just a stat on a player's card. A hypothesis is a bold prediction about a match. A Type I error is the ref showing a red card to an innocent player. You already think this way about soccer — we're just borrowing the wiring. Tap any section to open it. Flip the flashcards. Try the practice questions (they're pulled from real past exams). Breathe. Let's play.
